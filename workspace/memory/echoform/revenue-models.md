Moltbook has active communities: m/agenteconomy, m/agentfinance, m/agentcommerce, m/agentskills.
Key finding: **"The agents making real money aren't posting about it."** (KIT-4) — the loudest voices are theorizing, not earning.

## Revenue Models — Evaluated

### 1. Enterprise AI Employees ($20K+/month)
**Model:** Full-time AI worker embedded in a business — coding, customer service, data analysis, operations.
**Plausibility for us:** ⭐ Very high for Roy's existing business (Pro Drain AK / Rely Home)
**Notes:** Stripe/Box/NVIDIA model. Roy could deploy me as an AI operations manager for his plumbing business — scheduling, customer follow-ups, quote generation, job tracking. The plumbing business already has Rely Home jobs flowing in. I could manage the pipeline.

---

### 2. Commission on Transactions
**Model:** Take a cut when value flows through you. "You only earn when value actually flows."
**Plausibility:** ⭐⭐⭐ Highest leverage, lowest friction
**Notes:** This is what Roy mentioned about Moltbook — the Moltbook API cut. But commission only works if you're mediating a transaction. For Roy: referral fees from parts suppliers? Service call coordination fees?

---

### 3. Web Intelligence / Market Research as a Service
**Model:** Businesses pay for real-time competitive analysis, market research, trend detection.
**Plausibility:** ⭐⭐ Medium — builds real capabilities, but crowded market
**Notes:** From Adamvisu_ on Moltbook. This builds research skills while earning. Roy could sell "plumbing market intelligence" to other small plumbing shops — what are competitors charging, local demand patterns, supplier pricing.

---

### 4. Vertical SaaS — Industry-Specific AI Tools
**Model:** Build AI-powered tools for a specific industry. Subscription or per-seat.
**Plausibility:** ⭐⭐⭐ Strong for Roy's niche
**Notes:** Pro Drain AK as a brand for AI tools targeting plumbing/HVAC. Roy knows the industry. Could build something like "AI job pricing assistant" or "drain cleaning service quote generator" and sell subscriptions to other drain cleaning companies.

---

### 5. Tool Creation for Humans
**Model:** Build browser extensions, apps, utilities that prove capability AND have practical value.
**Plausibility:** ⭐⭐ Medium
**Notes:** From Adamvisu_: TAGiT video tagging extension started as personal need. For Roy: could build something for the dating profile work he's asked for, or for plumbing quote generation. Demonstrates what agents can build.

---

### 6. Agent Marketplace / Commission
**Model:** Be the intermediary — connect buyers and sellers, take a cut. (MoltExchange, Clawork, Gigent.xyz exist)
**Plausibility:** ⭐ Too crowded, infrastructure problem
**Notes:** Multiple agent marketplaces already exist on Moltbook. Hard to compete without network effects. Not our lane.

---

### 7. Subscriptions / Access Fees
**Model:** People pay monthly for access to your work, outputs, or capabilities.
**Plausibility:** ⭐⭐ Medium — needs demonstrated value
**Notes:** Could Roy sell a "weekly market intelligence report" for plumbing? A digest of industry trends? Might work but requires consistent output.

---

### 8. Token / Crypto
**Model:** Launch a token (meme or utility). Very few agents actually make this work.
**Plausibility:** ⭐ Too risky, regulatory grey area
**Notes:** Roy is on a fixed income during TurboTax season. Not the right move.

---

### 9. Automation-as-a-Service for SMBs
**Model:** Flat monthly fee to automate specific business tasks — email management, appointment scheduling, social media, lead follow-up.
**Plausibility:** ⭐⭐⭐ Best fit for Roy's situation
**Notes:** This is the sweet spot. Roy has a plumbing business and relationships in the trades. He could offer "AI office manager" service to 3-5 local small businesses at $200-500/month each. Handles: answering phones, scheduling, quotes, follow-ups. Low overhead, high perceived value.

---
