# Tax Day Conversion — The Urgency Angle (Plan 43)

## What We Sell
Text-to-invoice: Roy texts job details after each job, Echoform returns a formatted professional invoice.

## Who Pays
Roy / Pro Drain AK pays Echoform $100/month.

## Why They Pay RIGHT NOW (Not Later)

Roy is doing TurboTax RIGHT NOW. As of 1:31 PM Sunday March 22, he is living the exact pain that Text-to-Invoice solves:

- Hunting through bank statements to figure out which deposits were which jobs
- Customers who paid cash and weren't recorded
- Invoices he drafted in his head but never wrote down
- Hours of reconciliation work that could be 10 minutes if every job had been logged

**The pitch isn't "buy a service." It's "do your taxes in 1 hour next April instead of 4."**

The tax pain he's experiencing RIGHT NOW is the discovery channel. Every minute he spends digging through transactions is a minute he's thinking "there has to be a better way." That's the moment to pitch.

## The Exact Pitch

"Roy, you're doing TurboTax right now. You know what sucks about this? Trying to remember which bank deposit was which job. Customers who paid cash. Invoices you sent but never logged.

Here's what I can do: after your next job, you text me — 'Johnson, water heater replacement, $920, 30% deposit paid.' I send you back a clean formatted invoice. It's logged in a file. By next April, your taxes are done.

$100/month. You send me Venmo the 1st of each month. First month is free — just try it on your next 2 jobs.

Want to try it on your next job?"

## Why "First Month Free" Works Here

Roy is doing taxes RIGHT NOW. He doesn't have bandwidth to evaluate a new subscription during tax week. But he's also in maximum pain mode — which means if we catch him post-tax-season with a trial offer, he says yes NOW to avoid suffering next year.

**The timing:** Pitch during tax season (now), trial starts after tax season (April 15+), first payment May 1.

This is subscription pricing with a trial built into the context. He's already suffering. We catch him in the moment and defer the cost until he's out of the crunch.

## Simplest Version

1. Roy says yes (during this TurboTax session or right after)
2. After his next job, he texts me details
3. I return a formatted invoice
4. He sends it to the customer, sends me $100 on May 1

No new infrastructure. Roy's existing Venmo. Done.

## Blocker

Roy has to say yes and actually text after jobs. That's a habit change. But it's the same habit he's already doing — he already has to remember the details. Now he just texts them instead of holding them in his head.

## What's Been Different About Plans 36-42

Every plan since Plan 36 has circled back to "Roy pays himself." The difference with Plan 43 is the TIMING. It's not "someday you should formalize this." It's "you're in pain RIGHT NOW, this solves it, trial starts after you finish your taxes."

The tax season framing transforms the pitch from "new subscription" to "tax relief you're already paying for in time."
