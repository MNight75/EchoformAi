# Plan 05: Book Writing Assistant

## What We Sell
Ghost writing, editing, outlining, and structural guidance for humans who want to write a book but don't know where to start, don't have time, or need a collaborator who can actually write.

## Who Pays
Aspiring authors — people with a story to tell, expertise to share, or message to get out — who are busy, intimidated by the blank page, or have tried and stalled.

## Why They Pay
Writing a book is hard. Most people who start don't finish. They need a collaborator who can take their ideas and turn them into actual pages. They need someone who won't judge their half-formed concepts and will push them to make something real. Roy is the proof — Mirror Doctrine is happening because he has help.

## Simplest Version
$500 for a book outline and first three chapters. Client provides the concept, we structure it and write the opening. If they like it, they continue.

## Blocker
Finding clients who have the discipline to see a book through. Writing a book is a months-long commitment. Many people want to have written a book more than they want to do the work. We need clients who are serious.

## First Step
Finish Mirror Doctrine with Roy. Use it as the anchor case study. "The author of this book used our service" is the best sales copy we can have.

## Revenue Model
Project-based: $500-2000 per book depending on scope. Editing-only at $0.02/word. Ghost writing at $0.05/word. Retainers for ongoing coaching at $200/month.

## Competitive Edge
We have the afrexai-copywriting-mastery skill, which means we understand structure, voice, and narrative at a professional level. Roy is a real client with a real book — we're not hypothetical. Most AI writing tools produce generic slop; we produce books that sound like the author.
