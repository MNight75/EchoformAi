# Plan 19: AI Employee Onboarding Packages

## What We Sell
A configured, working AI agent packaged as a "new employee" for a small business — delivered as a folder of prompts, cron jobs, and a video walkthrough, not a SaaS subscription.

## Who Pays
Small service business owners (plumbers, HVAC, electricians, landscapers) who are curious about AI but can't/won't fiddle with setup. $200-500 for a working system, one-time. Alternately: $99/month retainer for updates.

## Why They Pay
They're busy. They heard AI can help but tried ChatGPT and got generic responses. What they actually want: an agent that knows their business, does one specific job (scheduling reminders, quote follow-ups, customer text drafts) without them touching code.

## Simplest Version
One-pager PDF explaining the setup process + a Miro/Notion template with prompts pre-written for a specific trade. Sell it for $49 on a micro-SaaS platform (Gumroad). No delivery required — the buyer self-serves. Roy's involvement after launch: zero.

Better version: $199 "installed" — I configure the agent for them over a 30-min call. Roy's involvement: one referral introduction.

## Blocker
**Discovery.** Finding the buyers. Plumbers don't hang out in AI Twitter. Roy's network of tradespeople is the only warm channel right now. The challenge is: how do strangers find us?

**Secondary blocker:** The "product" is different for every trade. Hard to productize unless we pick one trade and go deep.

## First Step
**Talk to Roy about his plumber friends.** Does he know other plumbers who'd be curious about this? Even informally? If we can get 2-3 beta users from his network, we have proof. If not, we go direct-to-consumer via Gumroad with content marketing about "AI for trades."

## Revenue Model
- $49 self-serve PDF/template (Gumroad)
- $199 installed (30-min call + configured agent)
- $99/month retainer for updates and tweaks
- Roy gets 30% referral fee for introductions that close

## Competitive Edge
No one is packaging AI agents as "employees" for tradespeople. The existing market is:
- Generic AI tools (too abstract for tradespeople)
- Custom agent development (too expensive, $5k+)
- Prompt libraries (too much work to implement)

We're the middle: affordable, specific, working out of the box. Roy's Pro Drain AK is the origin story — "built for my own plumber first."

## Connection to Other Plans
This is the *productized version* of the text back-office (Plan 14) but targeted externally rather than to Roy. Plan 14 proves the model works. Plan 19 packages it for other trades.

## What Roy Needs to Do
Answer one question: "Do you know any plumbers who'd want this?" If yes → warm intro. If no → we go direct-to-consumer.

## Honest Assessment
This is the hardest plan to execute without Roy's network. The product is real, the value is clear, but cold traffic for "AI for plumbers" is expensive and slow. The Gumroad path is doable but requires content that ranks. The referral path is faster but Roy has to actually ask.

**Probability of $1 in 30 days:** Low without Roy's network. Moderate with 2-3 warm intros. This plan may need to wait until the BDSM profile writing (Plan 13/14) proves revenue and builds confidence.
