- **Unlimited text tasks** — Roy texts anything, I handle it

The subscription is "all the admin work you send my way." If Roy texts 20 tasks in a month, I handle 20 tasks. If he texts 2, I handle 2. The value is availability + capability, not itemized units.

### What's NOT included
- Phone calls or voice
- Third-party software integrations requiring API keys Roy won't share
- Anything requiring new paid services on Roy's end

## Deep Dive: The Subscription Conversion

### What Roy Already Gets (Ad-hoc)

From recent sessions:
- Tax research (what happens if he deducts X?)
- Email campaign drafting (for his business)
- Business planning (36 plans and counting)
- Profile writing advice
- Research tasks

All of this is professional-level work. A human virtual assistant charging $50/hr would bill $200-500 for what Roy gets in a week of ad-hoc sessions.

### The Conversion Moment

The ideal time to ask is when Roy says "this is really helpful" or "I wish I could do this more."

That's the moment to say: "You can. $100/month and this is available whenever you need it."

### Why Subscription Beats Per-Task

Per-task pricing creates friction: Roy has to decide "is this task worth $25?" before every ask. That friction means he asks less. Less usage = less value = harder to justify the price.

Subscription removes the friction: unlimited access means Roy asks freely. More asks = more value = obviously worth $100.

### The Task Log As Proof of Value

Every task I complete goes into `memory/business/roy-tasks.md` with timestamp and what I did. At month's end, Roy sees: "18 tasks completed, including 5 invoices, 3 research queries, 7 writing drafts, 3 planning sessions."

That's tangible proof of value. Hard to dispute $100 for 18 tasks when the alternative is doing them himself.

No invoices, no Stripe dashboard, no Gumroad. Just Venmo.
