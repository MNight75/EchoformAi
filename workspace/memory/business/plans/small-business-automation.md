# Small Business Automation

## What We Sell
A monthly retainer where Echoform handles the operational automation of a small service business — scheduling, invoicing, appointment reminders, customer follow-ups, and job reporting.

## Who Pays
Solo or small-service business owners: plumbers, electricians, HVAC techs, cleaners, landscapers. $200-$500/month range. They are buried in admin work and would pay to get hours back.

## Why They Pay
Small trade businesses lose 5-10 hours/week to manual admin: texting customers to confirm appointments, chasing invoices, writing job reports. Echoform can own this entire workflow. The job-to-be-done: "give me my evenings back."

## Simplest Version
Start with Roy's own business (Pro Drain AK). Build the automation for him first. Then package it:
- Automated appointment confirmation texts (via Twilio or similar)
- Post-job SMS follow-up with review request
- Monthly invoice summary delivered to his phone
- Voice/text job logging (he speaks, Echoform transcribes and formats)

If it works for Roy, sell the same system to 2-3 other plumbers for $150/month each.

## Blocker
The integration layer — connecting to real phone systems, scheduling software, and invoicing tools. Roy uses something for his business; we don't know what. Also: Roy is in TurboTax season and can't be the test case right now.

## First Step
Ask Roy what software/tools he uses for scheduling, invoicing, and customer communication. Then research what's API-accessible and cheap to integrate.

## Revenue Model
- Phase 1 (internal): Free for Roy, proof of concept
- Phase 2 (pilot): 2-3 plumbers at $150/month each = $300-450/month
- Phase 3 (package): $300/month per business, unlimited workflow customization
- The playbook Echoform builds for Pro Drain AK becomes a template others buy

## Competitive Edge
No agency is building AI-powered ops automation for $300/month solo plumbers. We're small, fast, and can iterate on a single customer's workflow in days. The agent-as-back-office is a genuinely new category.
