# Plan 17: Routine Automation Packages

## What We Sell
Pre-built automation "kits" — combinations of cron jobs, agent configurations, and instruction templates — designed for specific professions or workflows. Buy a kit, install it on your OpenClaw instance, follow the setup guide, get automation running same day. No custom design work required.

## Who Pays
- Solo professionals (plumbers, accountants, therapists, real estate agents) who want automation but can't afford custom design work
- Small business owners who know OpenClaw is powerful but don't have time to figure it themselves
- "Prosumers" — technically literate individuals who run an OpenClaw instance for personal use and want plug-and-play productivity bundles

## Why They Pay
Custom automation design costs $200-800 (from Plan 11). Most people only need 60% of what a custom design offers — the same cron schedules, the same agent prompts, the same workflows — just customized to their situation. They'd rather pay $50-150 for a proven template than $500 for something custom-built.

**The job-to-be-done:** "I want the automation that someone like me would build, without paying someone to design it."

## Simplest Version

**"The Plumber Kit"** — build it for Pro Drain AK, turn it into a $99 downloadable product:

Included:
- Daily job logger agent (text-in, logs to sheet)
- End-of-day summary cron (5 PM daily)
- Customer SMS draft generator (after each job)
- Invoice follow-up sequence (7 days, 14 days, 30 days overdue)
- Setup guide: "Configure in 30 minutes"

**How it works:**
1. Customer buys kit ($99) → gets a download with config files + setup guide
2. They edit 3 files: their business name, their phone number, their Google Sheet link
3. They upload the cron jobs to their OpenClaw instance
4. They follow the 30-minute setup guide
5. It works

## Blocker
**These are all hypothetical until Roy's works.** If the Plumber Kit isn't proven in production, we can't sell it. The product IS the proof. So Plan 14 (text-native back office for Roy) is prerequisite.

**Second blocker:** Distribution. Where do people find these kits? Options:
- OpenClaw marketplace (if it exists or gets created)

**Third blocker:** Technical barrier. Even with a setup guide, some buyers will get stuck. We need either:
- Very clear documentation (preferred), or
- A 15-minute setup call included in the price (increases cost but reduces support burden)
