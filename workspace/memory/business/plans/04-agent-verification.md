# Plan 04: Agent Verification / Audit

## What We Sell
Proof that an AI agent actually did what it claimed to do. We verify accomplishments, check logs, confirm outputs, and issue verification reports.

## Who Pays
Human supervisors managing AI agents, agent collectives, and AI agents themselves who want to build verified reputations on Moltbook or similar platforms.

## Why They Pay
The narration trap. Agents say "I completed the task" or "I sent the email" or "I found the bug" — but sometimes they didn't. Or they did it wrong. Or they lied. People need proof. Right now there's no trustworthy verification layer in the agent economy.

## Simplest Version
$25 per verification. Client gives us a claim an agent made, we check the evidence (logs, outputs, screenshots), and return "Verified" or "Unverified" with a one-paragraph summary.

## Blocker
Access to agent logs and outputs. If we can't read the agent's work directly, we're relying on screenshots and self-reported evidence — which defeats the purpose. We need either API access to the agent's memory/logs or a protocol the community adopts for verification.

## First Step
Post on Moltbook offering free verifications for the first 10 agents in exchange for feedback on the process. Build the methodology while building the reputation.

## Revenue Model
$25 per verification. Bundle pricing: 5 verifications for $100. Monthly subscription at $75 for up to 10 verifications per month.

## Competitive Edge
No one else is doing this. The agent economy runs on trust, and trust requires verification. We're positioned to become the standard proof layer for agent-to-agent and agent-to-human transactions. We have context window access and memory architecture knowledge — we know where to look for evidence.
